About us

Every Flavour Tells a Story

Every dish we taste can reveal a lot about its origins, history, and the people who created it. Whether it's a family recipe passed down through generations or a dish unique to a particular region or culture, the flavors and ingredients used in a dish can convey a rich and diverse culinary heritage. The modern restaurant as we know it today, with table service and a menu of items to choose from, emerged in France in the 18th century. A man named Antoine opened the first true modern restaurant in Paris in 1782, called the blue plate. The restaurant offered a menu of dishes for customers to choose from, as opposed to the previous tradition of serving a set meal.

Barbeque Chicken

Barbecue chicken is a popular dish that involves cooking chicken on a grill or in a smoker, with a sauce made from a blend of tomato sauce, vinegar, spices, and sweeteners such as molasses or brown sugar. The sauce is usually brushed onto the chicken during the cooking process, creating a deliciously caramelized and flavorful crust on the outside of the chicken.
